# What's for Dinner? Product Specification

Status: Approved  
Last updated: June 27, 2026

## Product Goal

Help a family decide weekday dinners in under 10 minutes through balanced suggestions and a shared plan, then automatically produce the grocery list nobody wants to compile manually.

## Success Criterion

A family can complete a typical Monday-through-Friday dinner plan and receive its grocery list in under 10 minutes without manually compiling ingredients.

## Release 1 Scope

Release 1 delivers the complete online workflow:

- Verified user accounts with password reset, optional TOTP two-factor authentication, recovery codes, and passkeys.
- Shared, invite-only families with Head and member permissions.
- Family-owned ingredient and dish collections.
- Reviewed Markdown imports for existing dish collections.
- Ten balanced weekly dish suggestions when the collection permits.
- Monday-through-Friday dinner planning with main and alternative dishes.
- Automatically synchronized online grocery lists.
- A mobile-first dashboard and warm, family-oriented autumn design.
- Read-only historical plans with preserved dish snapshots.

The following are planned for later releases:

- Offline grocery-list caching and synchronization.
- Automatic live collaboration updates.
- Cross-family dish copying.
- Thirty-day family and account deletion recovery.
- Social login with explicit, secure account linking.

## Product Rules

### Accounts and Families

- Anyone may register with a name, email address, and password.
- Email verification is required before accessing application features.
- Users may reset their passwords.
- TOTP two-factor authentication is optional per user and supports compatible applications such as Google Authenticator.
- Recovery codes are single-use and may be regenerated by the account owner.
- Passkeys remain available as an additional sign-in method.
- A Head of Family cannot require another member to enable two-factor authentication.
- A user may belong to multiple families.
- Each family is a private shared workspace with its own dish collection, plans, and grocery lists.
- The user who creates a family becomes its Head.
- Each family has exactly one Head.
- Only the Head may change the family name or time zone, invite members, remove members, or initiate a Head transfer.
- All members may manage ingredients, dishes, weekly plans, and grocery lists.
- A non-Head member may leave a family at any time.
- A departing or removed member's contributions remain with the family.
- The Head must transfer leadership before leaving.
- A Head transfer requires the receiving member's acceptance. The former Head remains a regular member.

### Invitations

- Family membership is invitation-only.
- The Head invites a recipient by email address.
- Invitations are single-use and expire after seven days.
- The recipient must register or sign in with the invited email address before accepting or declining.
- The Head may revoke or resend a pending invitation.

### Ingredients

- Ingredients are reusable, family-owned records.
- Ingredient names are unique within a family, ignoring capitalization and extra whitespace.
- An ingredient may optionally be categorized as beef, poultry, pork, fish, or vegetable.
- Renaming an ingredient updates every dish that uses it.
- Before a global ingredient edit, the application shows how many dishes will be affected.
- An ingredient cannot be deleted while an active or archived dish references it.

### Dishes

- A dish stores a unique family-scoped name, an optional note, and one or more ingredients.
- Dishes do not store quantities, measurements, servings, or cooking instructions.
- Every dish has exactly one main-protein ingredient.
- The main ingredient's protein category determines the dish category.
- A dish may contain additional protein ingredients.
- Dish names are unique within a family, ignoring capitalization.
- Removing a dish archives it instead of erasing it.
- Archived dishes are excluded from normal selection and suggestions but remain visible in past plans.
- If a matching archived dish exists, members are prompted to restore or replace it instead of silently creating a duplicate.
- Members may search dishes by dish name or ingredient and filter by protein category or archive status.

### Markdown Importing

- Markdown importing is included in Release 1.
- Each `##` heading starts a dish.
- Bullet lines become ingredients.
- Exactly one ingredient must end with `(beef)`, `(poultry)`, `(pork)`, `(fish)`, or `(vegetable)`.
- The protein suffix is removed from the ingredient name and designates the main-protein ingredient.
- Non-bulleted text beneath a dish heading becomes the optional note.
- Importing first creates an editable preview without saving records.
- Members may correct dish names, ingredients, protein selections, categories, and notes in the preview.
- The preview flags malformed dishes, duplicate dish names, and ingredient conflicts.
- Exact ingredient matches ignore capitalization and extra spaces.
- Likely duplicates such as singular/plural forms or close spellings may be suggested but are never merged without explicit approval.
- Nothing is saved until the entire import passes validation and the member confirms it.

### Weekly Plans

- Each family has one plan per calendar week, covering Monday through Friday.
- Weeks and read-only boundaries use the family's configured time zone.
- Opening a week for the first time creates its plan and initial suggestions.
- Current and future plans are editable.
- Past plans and grocery lists are read-only.
- A weekday has exactly one main-dish slot and at most one alternative-dish slot.
- Filling all five main slots completes the week; no separate Ready action is required.
- Plans may remain incomplete while members are working on them.
- A dish may be assigned to one or more nonconsecutive weekdays.
- Occurrences after the first default to `Leftovers: [dish name]`.
- Members may remove the Leftovers designation when the dish will be cooked fresh again.
- `Eat Out` and `TV Dinner Night` are built-in main options.
- These built-in options add no ingredients, allow no alternative dish, and do not affect suggestion history.

### Suggestions

- Suggestions are individual dishes that members combine into dinner plans.
- The application targets ten unique suggestions: two beef, two poultry, two pork, two fish, and two vegetable.
- Archived dishes and dishes already scheduled for the selected week are excluded.
- Within each category, dishes not selected during the two immediately preceding calendar weeks are preferred.
- Both main and alternative dishes count as selected for the avoidance window.
- A dish scheduled on multiple nights counts once for that week.
- If a category lacks two available dishes, remaining slots are filled randomly from other categories.
- Recent dishes may be used when necessary to fill the set.
- If fewer than ten unique eligible dishes exist, the application shows as many as possible and prompts members to add dishes.
- A dish is never repeated merely to reach ten suggestions.
- Suggestions remain stable until a member explicitly regenerates them.
- Regeneration replaces all suggestions while leaving scheduled entries untouched.
- A regenerated set also excludes dishes already scheduled for that week.
- Suggestion cards display the dish name and protein category and may expand to show ingredients and the optional note.
- Members may schedule a suggested dish, select another saved dish, or create a new dish inline.

### Grocery Lists

- The weekly grocery list combines ingredients from all scheduled main and alternative dishes.
- Duplicate ingredient names appear once, ignoring capitalization and extra whitespace.
- Quantities and measurements are never shown.
- The list updates automatically whenever the weekly plan or a relevant active dish changes.
- Ingredients still required after recalculation preserve their checked state.
- Newly required ingredients appear unchecked.
- Generated ingredients no longer required are removed.
- Manually added items remain unchanged during recalculation.
- A manually added item belongs only to that family and week and does not become a reusable ingredient.
- Removing a generated ingredient suppresses it for the entire week.
- Suppressed ingredients remain removed after recalculation and are available in a Removed Items area for restoration.
- The default grocery list is flat and alphabetical.
- Unchecked items appear first.
- Checked items move into a collapsed Completed section.
- Generated items may expand to show which scheduled dishes require them.
- Manually added items are identified as manual.

### History

- Current and future scheduled entries use the latest applicable dish information.
- Editing a dish updates its current and future scheduled uses and recalculates active grocery lists.
- Past read-only weeks preserve dish names and ingredients as they existed when those weeks ended.

### Dashboard and Navigation

- The dashboard is the primary landing view.
- It shows the active family, a compact Monday-through-Friday dinner summary, grocery progress, and quick links to planning, groceries, and the dish collection.
- A family switcher is always accessible.
- Monday through Friday, the dashboard defaults to the current week.
- Saturday and Sunday, it defaults to the upcoming week.
- Members may navigate to other weeks manually.
- New families begin with an empty dish collection.
- A dismissible onboarding checklist shows progress toward two active dishes in every protein category.
- Members may begin planning before completing the checklist.

### Design and Accessibility

- The application is a mobile-first responsive web application.
- Planning and grocery shopping must be comfortable on a phone with large tap targets.
- Desktop layouts provide additional space for managing dishes and ingredients.
- Planning uses tap-based controls rather than requiring drag-and-drop.
- The visual direction is warm and family-oriented, using cream, terracotta, burnt orange, amber, deep olive, and dark brown.
- Light and dark themes follow the device preference by default and support a saved user override.
- Core workflows target WCAG 2.2 AA, including keyboard navigation, screen-reader support, visible focus states, adequate contrast, and accessible touch targets.
- Release 1 is English-only.

## Release 1 Non-Goals

- Quantities, measurements, or servings.
- Cooking instructions or full recipe management.
- Pantry inventory.
- Nutrition tracking.
- Budgets or prices.
- Store aisles or department organization.
- Grocery delivery integrations.
- Dish ratings, favorites, cuisine tags, or dietary filters.
- Native iOS or Android applications.
- Planning reminders or meal notifications.
- Social login.

## Shared Definition of Done

Every implementation spec must include:

- Authorization for every family-scoped action.
- Server-side validation.
- Appropriate models, factories, migrations, policies, and action classes.
- Pest tests covering the accepted behavior and permission boundaries.
- Livewire component tests for interactive workflows.
- Mobile browser coverage for critical user journeys where applicable.
- Loading, empty, validation, and failure states.
- Laravel Pint formatting for modified PHP files.
- The smallest relevant passing test command before completion.

Business rules belong in focused action or domain classes rather than Livewire components.

## Implementation Specs

### R1-01 — Verified Authentication

#### Outcome

Users have secure, verified accounts while retaining the starter application's supported authentication methods.

#### Requirements

- Activate mandatory email verification.
- Preserve registration, login, password reset, password confirmation, TOTP, recovery codes, and passkeys.
- Protect application routes with the `auth` and `verified` middleware.

#### Acceptance Tests

- An unverified user cannot access application features.
- A verified user can access the dashboard.
- Verification links work and can be resent subject to throttling.
- Password reset and password confirmation continue to work.
- Existing TOTP, recovery-code, and passkey tests remain passing.

### R1-02 — Family Creation and Switching

#### Outcome

Verified users can create and switch between isolated family workspaces.

#### Requirements

- Create a family with a name and time zone.
- Make the creator the Head and a family member.
- Allow a user to belong to multiple families.
- Persist the user's active family.
- Restrict family name and time-zone changes to the Head.

#### Acceptance Tests

- A verified user can create a family and becomes its Head.
- A user can belong to and switch between multiple families.
- Family data never appears in another family's queries or pages.
- Members cannot rename a family or change its time zone.
- The active family safely falls back when access is lost.

### R1-03 — Family Invitations

#### Outcome

Heads can securely invite people into their families.

#### Requirements

- Send an email-bound, single-use invitation that expires after seven days.
- Allow the recipient to register or sign in before accepting or declining.
- Require the authenticated account email to match the invited email.
- Allow the Head to revoke or resend a pending invitation.

#### Acceptance Tests

- Only the Head can create, revoke, or resend invitations.
- The invitation notification contains a valid acceptance link.
- Expired, revoked, consumed, and wrong-email invitations are rejected.
- Accepting creates one membership and cannot be repeated.
- Declining does not create a membership.
- Resending invalidates the previous invitation token.

### R1-04 — Membership and Head Transfer

#### Outcome

Families can safely manage membership and transfer leadership without becoming ownerless.

#### Requirements

- Allow members to leave and the Head to remove members.
- Preserve all family-owned contributions after departure.
- Allow the Head to offer leadership to an existing member.
- Require the receiving member to accept the transfer.
- Prevent the Head from leaving before transferring leadership.
- Keep family and account deletion controls unavailable until recovery is implemented.

#### Acceptance Tests

- Members cannot invite, remove others, or initiate transfers.
- A member may leave and immediately loses access.
- The Head may remove a member but cannot remove themselves.
- A pending transfer leaves the current Head unchanged.
- Acceptance atomically creates exactly one new Head.
- The previous Head remains a regular member.

### R1-05 — Ingredient Collection

#### Outcome

Families maintain a reusable, normalized ingredient collection.

#### Requirements

- Store family-scoped ingredient names and optional protein categories.
- Enforce normalized name uniqueness within a family.
- Propagate ingredient edits to connected dishes.
- Show affected-dish counts before global edits.
- Prevent deleting an ingredient referenced by any active or archived dish.

#### Acceptance Tests

- Capitalization and extra whitespace do not create duplicates.
- Different families may use the same ingredient name independently.
- Renaming updates connected dish displays and grocery generation.
- Protein-category changes reclassify dishes that use the ingredient as their main protein.
- Referenced ingredients cannot be deleted.

### R1-06 — Dish Collection

#### Outcome

Families can create, find, edit, archive, and restore valid dishes.

#### Requirements

- Store a unique family-scoped name, optional note, and ingredients.
- Require exactly one main-protein ingredient.
- Derive the dish category from that ingredient.
- Archive and restore instead of deleting.
- Search by dish name or ingredient.
- Filter by category and archive status.

#### Acceptance Tests

- A dish cannot be saved without ingredients and one main protein.
- A dish cannot designate multiple main proteins.
- Duplicate active and archived names receive the approved conflict behavior.
- Archived dishes disappear from active selection and suggestions.
- Search and filters remain family-scoped.
- Restoring makes a dish eligible again.

### R1-07 — Markdown Importer

#### Outcome

Members can safely import structured dish notes after reviewing the parsed data.

#### Requirements

- Parse headings, ingredient bullets, optional notes, and supported protein suffixes.
- Create an editable preview without persisting records.
- Flag invalid dishes, duplicate dish names, and ingredient conflicts.
- Suggest likely ingredient duplicates without merging automatically.
- Save the confirmed import atomically.

#### Acceptance Tests

- The approved sample format parses correctly.
- Each supported protein category parses correctly.
- Missing and multiple protein suffixes are flagged.
- Duplicate dish and ingredient conflicts require resolution.
- Canceling the preview writes nothing.
- Any failed record rolls back the full import.
- A valid confirmed import creates the expected dishes, ingredients, and relationships.

### R1-08 — Weekly Plan Foundation

#### Outcome

Each family has one predictable, time-zone-aware plan for every Monday-based week.

#### Requirements

- Create one plan per family and week start.
- Automatically create the plan and initial suggestions on first visit.
- Determine week boundaries using the family time zone.
- Permit edits only for current and future weeks.
- Treat missing prior plans as empty history.

#### Acceptance Tests

- Duplicate plans cannot be created for the same family and week.
- Plans with the same week may exist for different families.
- Time-zone boundaries correctly determine current and past weeks.
- Past plans reject mutations.
- First visit creates only one plan under concurrent requests.

### R1-09 — Suggestion Generator

#### Outcome

Each weekly plan receives a balanced, varied, and explainable set of dish suggestions.

#### Requirements

- Generate up to ten unique dishes.
- Target two dishes from each protein category.
- Exclude archived and already scheduled dishes.
- Prefer dishes absent from the prior two calendar weeks.
- Fill category shortages from other categories, then recent dishes.
- Preserve scheduled entries during regeneration.

#### Acceptance Tests

- A sufficiently populated collection returns exactly two dishes per category.
- No result contains an archived, scheduled, or duplicate dish.
- Main and alternative history both affect recent-dish preference.
- Multiple occurrences of one dish count once in history.
- Category shortages still produce the largest possible unique set.
- Collections smaller than ten never receive duplicate filler.
- Regeneration replaces suggestions without changing scheduled entries.

### R1-10 — Dinner Scheduling

#### Outcome

Members can quickly fill weekday dinner slots, including leftovers and non-cooking nights.

#### Requirements

- Support one main and at most one alternative entry per weekday.
- Schedule one dish across multiple, nonconsecutive days.
- Default later occurrences to Leftovers and allow a fresh-cooking override.
- Support Eat Out and TV Dinner Night as special main entries.
- Determine completeness from the five main slots.

#### Acceptance Tests

- A second main or alternative entry for the same day is rejected.
- A dish can be scheduled across nonconsecutive weekdays.
- Later occurrences default to Leftovers.
- A member can remove the Leftovers designation.
- Special entries add no alternative and no ingredients.
- Five main entries, including special entries, complete the week.

### R1-11 — Historical Dish Snapshots

#### Outcome

Active plans follow dish edits while past plans remain historically accurate.

#### Requirements

- Store the dish information required by each scheduled entry.
- Refresh current and future scheduled snapshots after dish edits.
- Preserve past scheduled snapshots.
- Reconcile affected active grocery lists after updates.

#### Acceptance Tests

- Editing a dish updates current and future scheduled displays.
- Editing ingredients updates active grocery lists.
- The same edit does not change a past plan or grocery list.
- Archiving a dish does not erase historical snapshots.

### R1-12 — Grocery Reconciliation Engine

#### Outcome

Every active plan has an accurate grocery list that preserves intentional user state.

#### Requirements

- Derive ingredients from scheduled main and alternative dishes.
- Merge normalized duplicates.
- Preserve check state for retained items.
- Add new items unchecked and remove obsolete generated items.
- Preserve manual items.
- Retain weekly suppression tombstones until restoration or week end.
- Track source scheduled dishes.
- Reconcile atomically.

#### Acceptance Tests

- Duplicate ingredients become one grocery item.
- Retained items preserve checked state after a plan change.
- New ingredients are unchecked.
- Obsolete generated items disappear.
- Manual items survive plan changes.
- Suppressed ingredients do not reappear after recalculation.
- Restoring a suppressed ingredient returns it with correct sources.
- Source attribution includes all requiring dishes.

### R1-13 — Grocery-List Interface

#### Outcome

Members can use the generated list directly while shopping online.

#### Requirements

- Display unchecked items alphabetically.
- Move checked items into a collapsed Completed section.
- Support checking, unchecking, manual additions, removal, suppression, and restoration.
- Expand generated items to show source dishes.
- Identify manual items.
- Authorize every action against the active family.

#### Acceptance Tests

- Every interface action persists the expected state.
- Generated removal creates suppression; manual removal deletes the manual item.
- Completed items render in the correct section.
- Source expansion displays the correct dishes.
- Members cannot mutate another family's grocery list.
- The core flow works at a phone-sized browser viewport without JavaScript errors.

### R1-14 — Dashboard and Onboarding

#### Outcome

Members immediately see the information and next actions needed to plan and shop.

#### Requirements

- Show active family, weekday dinner summary, grocery progress, and quick links.
- Keep the family switcher accessible.
- Default to the current week on weekdays and upcoming week on weekends.
- Show an empty-family checklist with progress toward two dishes per category.
- Allow each member to dismiss the checklist.

#### Acceptance Tests

- Dashboard data is scoped to the active family.
- Grocery progress counts active unchecked and total items correctly.
- Default week changes correctly at the weekend in the family time zone.
- Checklist progress reflects active dish categories.
- Checklist dismissal is stored per member and family.
- Switching families refreshes all dashboard content.

### R1-15 — Mobile Design and Release Acceptance

#### Outcome

Release 1 provides a cohesive, accessible, phone-first experience covering the complete online workflow.

#### Requirements

- Apply the approved warm autumn visual system in light and dark themes.
- Provide large touch targets and tap-based planning controls.
- Preserve all functionality on mobile while expanding collection management on desktop.
- Meet WCAG 2.2 AA fundamentals across core workflows.

### R1-16 — Sunday-Anchored Weekend Plan Expansion

#### Outcome

Weekly plans cover a full Sunday-through-Saturday week, so a family's entire week of dinners and its single grocery list match the way the family actually shops and cooks — without changing what "completing" a week means and without altering historical 5-day plans.

#### Decisions Locked

- The week is Sunday through Saturday for all families. There is no per-family week-start setting; flexibility there is explicitly out of scope and would be revisited only by rebuilding.
- `weekday` is stored as ISO-8601 day-of-week (1 = Monday … 7 = Sunday). The weekend days added are Saturday = 6 and Sunday = 7.
- The application's week boundary and ordering are Sunday-first. ISO numbering is the storage vocabulary only and does not make Monday the first day of the week.

#### Requirements

- Add an `includes_weekend` boolean to the weekly-plan model, migration, and factory, defaulting to `true`. It distinguishes Sunday-start 7-day plans from frozen legacy 5-day plans.
- Backfill existing plans: set `includes_weekend` to `true` where the plan's week is current-or-future in the family's time zone, and `false` otherwise. Past plans stay 5-day and are never retroactively re-sliced.
- Define the week as Sunday through Saturday in a single, shared boundary helper, treating Sunday (7) as the first day and Saturday (6) as the last. Do not rely on Carbon's default `startOfWeek()`. All current-week, read-only-cutoff, and recent-avoidance-window logic uses this helper. Boundaries remain time-zone-aware per family.
- A weekend-enabled plan covers all seven days, ordered Sunday (7), Monday (1), Tuesday (2), Wednesday (3), Thursday (4), Friday (5), Saturday (6). Legacy plans remain the five weekdays, Monday through Friday.
- Update server-side scheduling validation to accept `weekday` 6 and 7 only when the plan's `includes_weekend` is `true`, and reject them otherwise.
- Extend per-day scheduling (one main and at most one alternative; Leftovers default; `Eat Out` and `TV Dinner Night`) to Saturday and Sunday on 7-day plans.
- Keep suggestion generation at ten dishes. Weekend-scheduled dishes count as already-scheduled for exclusion and recent-avoidance, evaluated in Sunday-anchored weeks.
- Keep completeness derived solely from the five weekday (Monday–Friday) main slots. Saturday and Sunday are optional and never affect or block completion.
- Remove the weekend → upcoming-week dashboard rule. The dashboard defaults to the current week every day, including the weekend.
- Render the dashboard summary, planning grid, and read-only past view in Sunday-first order for the correct day count per plan (seven days for weekend-enabled plans, five for legacy plans).
- Keep the planning grid comfortable at phone width and accommodate seven Sunday-first columns on desktop.

#### Acceptance Tests

- A new plan is created with `includes_weekend` true, ordered Sunday-first, and accepts Saturday (6) and Sunday (7) entries.
- Scheduling `weekday` 6 or 7 on a legacy 5-day plan is rejected by server-side validation.
- A past, pre-change plan continues to render and behave as five Monday-through-Friday days and is unaffected by the migration.
- The backfill sets current and future existing plans to 7-day and leaves past plans 5-day.
- The week-boundary helper treats Sunday as the first day, so a Sunday and its following Saturday resolve to the same plan, grocery list, and read-only state.
- A week is complete once its five weekday main slots are filled, regardless of weekend slots.
- Leaving both weekend slots empty never blocks completion and never appears as missing.
- A dish scheduled only on a weekend appears on the grocery list and counts once in suggestion history within the Sunday-anchored week.
- The recent-avoidance window counts in Sunday-anchored weeks.
- The dashboard defaults to the current week on Saturday and Sunday and shows weekend dinners.
- Every affected action is covered by authorization checks scoped to the active family.

#### Amendments to Existing Specs

- R1-08 (Weekly Plan Foundation): week coverage becomes Sunday through Saturday for weekend-enabled plans; the week boundary is Sunday-anchored via the shared helper; add `includes_weekend`. This supersedes the original "Monday-based week" language for new plans, while legacy plans retain their original Monday-through-Friday behavior.
- R1-09 (Suggestion Generator): weekend-scheduled dishes participate in exclusion and recent-avoidance; the avoidance window is Sunday-anchored; the suggestion count stays ten.
- R1-10 (Dinner Scheduling): per-day rules extend to Saturday (6) and Sunday (7); completeness remains the five weekday main slots.
- R1-12 / R1-13 (Grocery): no rule change; weekend dishes contribute to the single weekly list automatically once scheduled.
- R1-14 (Dashboard): remove the weekend default-week rule; the summary spans seven Sunday-first days for 7-day plans.
- R1-15 (Mobile): the planning grid and dashboard summary are verified at seven Sunday-first days on phone and desktop.
